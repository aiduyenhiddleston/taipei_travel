A Taipei 101 Visitor’s Guide – Experience Taiwan’s Iconic Skyscraper

Post by 明順 | 2026-04-15

When talking about Taipei, one of the first landmarks that comes to mind is Taipei 101. Standing tall in the heart of the city, this iconic skyscraper is not only a symbol of modern Taiwan but also a must-visit destination for travelers from around the world. Whether you are interested in architecture, shopping, or simply enjoying breathtaking views, Taipei 101 has something for everyone.

Taipei 101 was once the tallest building in the world, holding that title from 2004 to 2010. Even today, it remains one of the most impressive skyscrapers globally. The building stands at 508 meters and features 101 floors above ground, which is how it got its name. Its unique design is inspired by traditional Chinese architecture, resembling a bamboo stalk, symbolizing growth, prosperity, and resilience.

Highlights of the Experience

One of the most popular attractions in Taipei 101 is the observatory. Located on the 89th floor, the indoor observation deck offers a panoramic view of Taipei City. Visitors can enjoy a 360-degree view, seeing everything from bustling streets to distant mountains. On clear days, the scenery is absolutely stunning, making it a perfect spot for photography lovers.

Another interesting feature of Taipei 101 is its massive wind damper, located between the 87th and 92nd floors. This giant golden sphere weighs over 600 tons and helps stabilize the building during strong winds and earthquakes. It is one of the few skyscrapers in the world that allows visitors to see this engineering marvel up close, making the experience both educational and fascinating.

Besides sightseeing, Taipei 101 is also a shopping paradise. The lower floors house a luxury shopping mall featuring international brands and high-end restaurants. Food lovers can taste both local Taiwanese dishes and international cuisine, often while overlooking the city skyline.

Taipei 101 is especially magical at night. As the sun sets, the building lights up, becoming a shining beacon in the city. In addition, it plays an important role during New Year’s Eve celebrations, hosting a spectacular fireworks show that attracts visitors from all over the world.

How to get there: The building is located in the Xinyi District and is easily accessible by Taipei MRT Red Line at Taipei 101/World Trade Center Station.
